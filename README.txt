The Championship Cube:
A Front Office Framework for Building NBA Champions - Submitted to the MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference 2027
---------------------------------------------
I started this project because I got tired of hearing championships explained as if talent alone was the answer. The Championship Cube is my attempt to break down what actually separates NBA champions from teams that look good on paper but never quite get there.

Instead of treating winning as one big vague idea, I look at it through six connected faces — the roster, the players, the coach, the front office, the controllable external factors, and the uncontrollable ones. Each face answers a different question about how a champion is built, and the point is to show how those pieces interact rather than stand alone.

This is a research project built around every NBA champion and Finals runner-up from 1990 to 2026, using Basketball Reference as the primary data source. Python, spreadsheets, and statistical modelling turn a basketball argument into something measurable, testable, and reproducible.
---------------------------------------------
The Six Faces:
Team Face — What the roster looked like, how it was built, and how it performed relative to its era.
Player Face — Which players drove value, how they were acquired, and whether they fit their role in the hierarchy.
Coach Face — How the coach built the system, established culture, and matched the roster's identity.
Front Office Face — How the team drafted, traded, signed players, and shaped the long-term plan.
Controllable External Face — Load management, scheduling, and sports science decisions.
Uncontrollable External Face — Injuries, variance, and how well a team absorbed bad breaks.
